Sharyn Brand is an installation artist and collaborator focusing on sound-based urban ethnography, navigating the complexity of the world by uncovering the hidden and forgotten. In 2019 Sharyn received a Graduate Diploma of Music in Sonic Art from Te Kōkī, New Zealand School of Music Victoria University, Wellington.
Career highlights: Movement Extract (2019), Te Pātaka Toi, Adam Art Gallery Wellington NZ; Colour Labyrinth (2018/19), ArtPlay’s New Ideas Lab; Reflection (2017), Phantasmagoria, Bogong Sound Village; Maybe_Together’s Small Voices Louder Perth International Festival (2017); 
Renae Shadler’s Can You See What We See? Junction Arts Festival (2016); Blood.Sex.Tears. Women Of the World (2017).
